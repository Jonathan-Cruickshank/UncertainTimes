Police Cadet Lancaster and Morecambe Police Cadets September 2012 – September 2015 Every Wednesday night I and about 20 other people my age met in a classroom at the local College to learn about what it meant to be a police officer. Almost every week we had a guest speaker come in from a different police branch to tell us about their jobs and how they did them. Routinely we would drill a police physical exam. We were responsible for maintaining our own health and I followed a workout regimen I planned myself. Outside the classroom we were taught how to properly use two-way radios and did lots of community outreach, both running our own stalls to show the public who we were and as volunteers. Once when we had our own stall at the local car boot sale the Rotary Club running it realised they didn’t have enough people to direct traffic, and since we had prior experience and hi-vis jackets we were quickly briefed on the one-way system and car park they had set up and then allowed to direct cars coming and going.
